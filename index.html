Samosa Trivia

When did Samosa first appear in the India?

The samosa has a Central Asian origin. The earliest mention of the samosa was by Abbasid-era poet Ishaq al-Mawsili, praising the sanbusaj. Recipes are found in 10th–13th-century Arab cookery books, under the names sanbusak, sanbusaq, and sanbusaj, all deriving from the Persian word sanbosag.A samosa is a fried or baked pastry with a savory filling, including ingredients such as spiced potatoes, onions, peas. It may take different forms, including triangular, cone, or half-moon shapes, depending on the region.Samosas are often accompanied by chutney. (Source: Wikipedia)
